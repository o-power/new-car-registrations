New Private Car Registrations in Ireland

The switch to used imports

New car sales dropped sharply in 2009 as a result of the banking crisis. Post-2009 new car sales gradually rose, approaching their pre-2009 levels in 2016. However, new car sales dropped in 2017 and again in 2018 as the popularity of used imports increased.

The Rise of Hyundai

Hyundai has gone from ranking 9th in 2008 with new car sales of 5,355 to ranking 3rd in 2018 with sales of 11,633 units. The Hyundai Tucson, a compact SUV, has driven this increase and was the most popular new car model in 2018 with sales of 4,026 units.

The Irish Car Park in 2018

There figure has a rectangle for every car model sold in Ireland in 2018. The size of the rectangle is proportional to the number of units sold. Hover over the figure to explore the popularity of each model. The Hyundai Tucson was the most popular model in 2018 with sales of 4,026 units.

The Decline of Gold and Beige

Gold and Beige were the 6th and 8th most popular car colours in 2010 with sales of 4,279 and 1,072 units, respectively. In 2018, however, no gold cars were registered and only 1 beige car was registered. In contrast, white has risen to the 3rd most popular colour with sales of 18,268 in 2018, up from 1,839 in 2010. Grey is the most popular car colour. Multi-coloured cars (i.e. cars with two or more colours like white and black) have seen a rise in popularity although this may be a consequence of more exact reporting of car colours. Similarly, silver/aluminium has tumbled in popularity but this may be a consequence of the colour being now reported as grey.

Dublin, Cork and Waterford top new car sales per 100 adults

In 2018, 5.2 new cars were sold per 100 adults in Dublin. Next highest were Cork and Waterford with 3.9 new cars per 100 adults. Leitrim and Longford sold the fewest new cars at just 2.1 new cars per 100 adults. The adult population of each county was taken from the 2016 census with the ages of 19 and under excluded.
